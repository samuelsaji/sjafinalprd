St Thomas Auditorium


Location   :   Trivandrum
Year           :   2015

>

Adding a new building into an existing campus always is an opportunity to better the relationships between the spaces.

The connecting landscape:
The auditorium with its sloped raking, would sit quite well within the sloping terrain. Our thought however, was to see the opportunity of this slope extending up till the road in the form of a Plaza.

The Auditorium: The Auditorium is designed considering optimum vision angles and sight lines. A stage with a proscenium opening of 15m, with two side stages of 5mx5m is given. There is also a catwalk access for lighting/ scene controls, orchestra pit, green rooms, and light-sound-projection room. The present design considers a seating of 1700 on two levels.

Parallel events can happen within the gallery. The space can also serve as an extension to the main auditorium, accommodating the crowd spill over.

The stepped plaza becomes a place that links the auditorium to the other school buildings around it. The plaza becomes a meeting point or children’s play area, also absorbing the crowds, during events. The plaza therby connects the building to its landscape, forming a seamless edge
