Sariga Arun

IIA State Award-Commendation 2016

Location   :   Ernakulaam
Year          :   2021
Status       :  Completed

Sariga Arun Residence is a 2500 sq. ft house, in 5 cents of land for a joint family. A mother, her two sons, their wives, their young children and their visiting friends make up the users.

>

The design process started off with leaving 3m setbacks on two sides (instead of the mandatory 3m in the front).

It was our observation that, in the case of tight urban plots such as these , as much as satisfying the client requirements of built space, one needed to look beyond that and increase open to sky space in the site by keeping the built as least as possible.

Today, while the extra open space left in the site becomes not only the garden but also a space for two cars and 2 bikes and a guest car if needed. However restrictive this meant for the planning, care was taken to ensure communicable spaces in the house, to engage the family through flexible spaces and connecting sections.

Roadway leading to entry

An ideal sitting for the young children, and by its position at one end of the dining area- it accommodates the spill over from dining and completes the conversation circle.

The thinna here is a raised platform, 30cm higher than the immediate dining room level and 60cm higher than the sunken family room level.

The study room on the first floor and the bedroom window looks into the thinna. So it works as a place where the family comes together to do different activities as they please. The changing sunlight marks the passage of time, and the activity the space

It hosts a number of activities: one end becomes the TV wall, other the Pooja area with a sunken planter box

The thinna reveals a pull out bed that wheels out into the family room. The space converts into a guest room once the curtains are pulled down and the bed is out. The adjoining common toilet adds to the purpose. A quick solution to accommodate frequent visitors.

The Courtyard & The Thinna

While we were working out the courtyard in Sariga Arun Residence, we wanted a courtyard that was alive. One which could take in the life around- as against the static, pebble laid, planted court which has become part of the contemporary kitsch and only looks good. At the Sariga Arun residence- while bringing in light and taking out the hot air through its skylights, and an added relief of green- the courtyard was meant to bring the activities of the house together under its umbrella of light This was done by merging the two most important spatial devices of traditional Kerala architecture - the nadumuttam and the thinna

In the sense that the thinna becomes a stage to the courtyard.

This being a hindu family, prayers are offered every day- morning and evening. The mother reads out prayers sitting cross legged on the thinna, and children gather around. The raised nature of the thinna from the surrounding floor makes the activity of prayer feel dignified: in a sense, raising the divine over the profane.

The volume of the stair room connects spaces through subtle sections -As one climbs up the stairs, a long slit opening connects the space to the living room , and a small window (kilivathil) connects it to the master bedroom. The stair room is naturally lit from the open terrace at south west corner but the light source beyond is kept hidden, only revealing as one turns the landing. From there the scale dramatically changes :opening out to the sky, and the rooms on the first floor. There is a deliberate change in scale and the quality of light; at the turn of direction.

The railing borders the double height space on one side, and skirts around the built in seat in the study room and corridor on the first floor - an effective back rest and suited to keep children safe
