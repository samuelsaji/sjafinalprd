Stone house

IA&B Young designer of the Year Award 2012, IIA State Award-Commendation 2012

Location   :   Munnar
Year          :   2013
Status       :  Completed

“ The feel of crushing the dry fallen leaves against the soft grass, the murmur of wind hurrying past the tall trees, or the incessant sound of cicadas, the dancing shadows of leaves on white plaster. The eyes are drawn angling up along the soaring roof while the hand touches the roughness & impermeability of cold stone, the smooth transparency of delicate glass on which the trees reflect on. The warmth of fire to save the chill of the night wind.

>

The building had to be sited in a clearing, already made in a steeply sloping plot. Responding to the lie of the land, it was decided to have an inclined profile for the building & to keep the roofline low, thereby reducing the impact of intrusion into the pristine landscape. The house buttresses itself against the existing rocks; hardly rising above the contour line.

Varying scales & light quality serve to be markers of space. - From the intimate scale of the verandah to the growing scale of the living space & mezzanine to the stairwell.

The scale at the stairwell is soaring ........ narrow and high, with natural light subtly thrown in from the sides & roof. As one walks up through this space, the circulation weaves through the building and around it; and a sense of construction and release is made to be felt. Roof level slits given on the North West stone walls track the light of the afternoon sun, and also aid in naturally ventilating the space by stack effect. Vistas of the existing rocks in the site are framed along this movement corridor.

The program requirements included 3 bedrooms for the family, a caretaker’s room, pantry, and living - dining spaces. The volume under the sloping roof is divided into the verandah, where one enters the building; the living/dining space, a mezzanine space and the stairs. A stone fireplace & chimney tower serves to bind all these spaces under the roof. The stairwell segregates the bedrooms from the common spaces.

Abundant supply of stone in close vicinity & skilled labor resulted in the use of stone load bearing walls. Natural materials like tiled roofs, Kota, Kadappa stones for flooring were used.
